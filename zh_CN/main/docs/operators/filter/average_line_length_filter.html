average_line_length_filter#

Filter to keep samples with average line length within a specific range.

This operator filters out samples based on their average line length. It keeps samples where the average line length is between the specified minimum and maximum values. The average line length is calculated as the total text length divided by the number of lines. If the context is provided, it uses precomputed lines from the context. The computed average line length is stored in the 'avg_line_length' key in the stats field.

This example demonstrates the basic usage of the AverageLineLengthFilter. The operator is set to keep samples with an average line length between 10 and 20 characters. It calculates the average line length by dividing the total number of characters in each sample's text by the number of lines. вңЁ explanation и§ЈйҮҠ#

This example illustrates how the AverageLineLengthFilter works when context information is provided. The operator still filters samples based on their average line length being between 10 and 20 characters. However, it uses precomputed line information from the context for its calculations. As a result, the same two samples, 'a v s e e f g a qkc' and 'emojiиЎЁжғ…жөӢиҜ•дёӢрҹҳҠпјҢрҹҳё31231\n', are kept as they meet the criteria.
